सर्वदर्विहोमाणामेष कल्पः १
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

1. This is the rite for all Darvi-sacrifices.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

सर्वदर्विहोमाणामेष कल्पः १
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

मन्त्रान्ते नित्यः स्वाहाकारः २
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

2. [^1]  At the end of the Mantras constantly the word Svāhā (is pronounced).

[^1]:  3, 2. Gobhila I, 9, 25.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

मन्त्रान्ते नित्यः स्वाहाकारः २
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

अमन्त्रासु । अमुष्मै स्वाहा । इति यथादेवतम् ३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

3. [^2]  (Oblations) for which no Mantras are prescribed (are made merely with the words), 'To such and such (a deity) svāhā!' - according to the deity (to whom the oblation is made).

[^2]:  Sāṅkhāyana I, 9, 18.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

अमन्त्रासु । अमुष्मै स्वाहा । इति यथादेवतम् ३
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

भूः । भुवः । सुवः । इति व्याहृतिभिर्जुहोत्येकैकशः समस्ताभिश्च ४
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

4. [^3]  He sacrifices with the Vyāhṛtis, 'Bhūḥ! Bhuvaḥ! Suvaḥ!' - with the single (three Vyāhṛtis) and with (the three) together.

[^3]:  Śāṅkhāyana I, 12, 12. 13; Gobhila I, 9, 27. As to suvaḥ, the spelling of the Taittirīyas for svaḥ, see Indische Studien, XIII, 105.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

भूः । भुवः । सुवः । इति व्याहृतिभिर्जुहोत्येकैकशः समस्ताभिश्च ४
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

आयुर्दा अग्ने । इत्येषा । आयुर्दा देव जरसं गृणानो घृतप्रतीको घृतपृष्ठो अग्ने । घृतं पिबन्नमृतं चारु गव्यं पितेव पुत्रं जरसे नयेमम् । स्वाहा ५
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

5. [^4]  (The Mantras for the two chief oblations are), the (verse), 'Life-giving, Agni!' (Taitt. Brāhmaṇa I, 2, 1, 11), (and),

[^4]:  5, 6. In the second Mantra we should read vṛṇāno instead of gṛṇāno; comp. Atharva-veda II, 13, 1. As to the Mantras that follow, comp. Pāraskara I, 2, 8; Taittirīya Āraṇyaka IV, 20, 3. - Regarding the Mantra tvam Agne ayāsi (sic), comp. Taitt. Brāh. II, 4, 1, 9; Āśvalāyana-Śrauta-sūtra I, 11, 13; Kātyāyana-Śrauta-sūtra XXV, 1, 11; Indische Studien, XV, 125.

'Life-giving, O god, choosing long life, thou whose face is full of ghee, whose back is full of ghee, Agni, drinking ghee, the noble ambrosia that comes from the cow, lead this (boy) to old age, as a father (leads) his son. Svāhā!'

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

आयुर्दा अग्ने । इत्येषा । आयुर्दा देव जरसं गृणानो घृतप्रतीको घृतपृष्ठो अग्ने । घृतं पिबन्नमृतं चारु गव्यं पितेव पुत्रं जरसे नयेमम् । स्वाहा ५
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

इमं मे वरुण । तत्त्वा यामि । त्वं नो अग्ने । स त्वं नो अग्ने । त्वमग्ने अयास्यया सन्मनसा हितः । अया सन्हव्यमूहिषेया नो धेहि भेषजम् । स्वाहा । प्रजापते । इत्येषा ६
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

6. (Then follow oblations with the verses),

'This, O Varuṇa' (Taitt. Saṃh. II, 1, 11, 6),

'For this I entreat thee' (Taitt. Saṃh., loc. cit.),

'Thou, Agni' (Taitt. Saṃh. II, 5, 12, 3),

'Thus thou, Agni' (Taitt. Saṃh., loc. cit.),

'Thou, Agni, art quick. Being quick, appointed (by us) in our mind (as our messenger), thou who art quick, earnest the offering (to the gods). O quick one, bestow medicine on us! Svāhā!' - (and finally) the (verse),

'Prajāpati!' (Taitt. Saṃh. I, 8, 14, 2).

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

इमं मे वरुण । तत्त्वा यामि । त्वं नो अग्ने । स त्वं नो अग्ने । त्वमग्ने अयास्यया सन्मनसा हितः । अया सन्हव्यमूहिषेया नो धेहि भेषजम् । स्वाहा । प्रजापते । इत्येषा ६
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

य᳓द् अस्य क᳓र्मणो᳓ ऽत्य᳓रीरिचं  
य᳓द् वा न्यू᳙नम् इहा᳓करम् ।  
अग्नि᳓ष् ट᳓त् स्विष्टकृ᳓द् विद्वा᳓न्थ्  
स᳓र्वँ स्वि᳙ष्टँ सु᳓हुतं᳓ क᳓रो᳓तु᳓ मे᳓  
स्वा᳓हा ।
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

यद॑स्य॒ कर्म॒णोऽत्यरी॑रिचं॒ यद्वा॒ न्यू॑नमि॒हाक॑रम् ।  
अ॒ग्निष्टत्स्वि॑ष्ट॒कृद्वि॒द्वान्त्सर्व॒ग्ग्॒ स्वि॑ष्टँ॒ सुहु॑तं करोतु मे ।
</details>

अग्नये स्विष्टकृते  
सुहुतहुते सर्वहुत  
आहुतीनां कामानां समर्धयित्रे स्वाहा । 

इत्युत्तरार्धपूर्वार्धेसंसक्तामितराभिराहुतीभिर्जुहोति ७
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

7. [^5]  (With the verse), 'What I have done too much in this sacrifice, or what I have done here deficiently, all that may Agni Sviṣṭakṛt, he who knows it, make well sacrificed and well offered for me. To Agni Sviṣṭakṛt, the offerer of well-offered (sacrifices), the offerer of everything, to him who makes us succeed in our offerings and in our wishes, svāhā!' - he offers (the Sviṣṭakṛt oblation) over the easterly part of the northerly part (of the fire), separated from the other oblations.

[^5]:  Āśvalāyana-Gṛhya I, 10, 23; Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa XI V, 9, 4, 24.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

यदस्य कर्मणीत्यरीरिचं यद्वा न्यूनमिहाकरम् । अग्निष्टत्स्विष्टकृद्विद्वान्सर्वं स्विष्टं सुहुतं करोतु मे । अग्नये स्विष्टकृते सुहुतहुते सर्वहुत आहुतीनां कामानां समर्धयित्रे स्वाहा । इत्युत्तरार्धपूर्वार्धेसंसक्तामितराभिराहुतीभिर्जुहोति ७
</details>

अत्रैके जयाभ्यातानान्राष्ट्रभृत इत्युपजुह्वति पुरस्तात्स्विष्टकृतः ८

8. [^6]  Here some add as subordinate oblations, before the Sviṣṭakṛt, the Jaya, Abhyātāna, and Rāṣṭrabhṛt (oblations).

10. [^8]  The Abhyātāna (oblations) with (the eighteen Mantras), 'Agni is the lord of beings; may he protect me' (&c.).

[^8]:  Taittirīya Saṃhitā III, 4, 5.

[^9]:  See the end of the section quoted in the last note.

पितरः पितामहाः । इति प्राचीनावीती जुहोत्युपतिष्ठते वा १२
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

12. [^10]  With (the last of the Abhyātāna formulas) 'Fathers! Grandfathers!' he sacrifices or performs worship, wearing the sacrificial cord over his right shoulder.

[^10]:  'He performs worship with that Mantra, wearing the sacrificial cord over his right shoulder, to the Manes. According to others, he worships Agni. But this would stand in contradiction to the words (of the Mantra).' Mātṛdatta.

</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

पितरः पितामहाः । इति प्राचीनावीती जुहोत्युपतिष्ठते वा १२
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

ऋताषाडृतधामा । इति राष्ट्रभृतः । पर्यायमनुद्रुत्य । तस्मै स्वाहा । इति पूर्वामाहुतिं जुहोति । ताभ्यः स्वाहा । इत्युत्तराम् १३
</details>

<details><summary>Oldenberg</summary>

13. [^11]  The Rāṣṭrabhṛt (oblations he sacrifices) with (the twelve Mantras), 'The champion of truth, he whose law is truth.' After having quickly repeated (each) section, he sacrifices the first oblation with (the words), 'To him svāhā!' the second (oblation) with (the words), 'To them svāhā!'

[^11]:  Taittirīya. Saṃhitā III, 4, 7. 'To him' (tasmai) is masculine, 'to them' (tābhyaḥ) feminine. The purport of these words will be explained best by a translation of the first section of the Rāṣṭrabhṛt formulas: 'The champion of truth, he whose law is truth, Agni is the Gandharva. His Apsaras are the herbs; "sap" is their name. May he protect this power of holiness and this worldly power. May they protect this power of holiness and this worldly power. To him svāhā! To them svāhā!'

</details>
