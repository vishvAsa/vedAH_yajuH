>

  1. Food (offered to guests) which is mixed with milk procures the reward of an Agniṣṭoma-sacrifice. Food mixed with clarified butter procures the reward of an Ukthya, food mixed with honey the reward of an Atirātra, food accompanied by meat the reward of a Dvādaśāha, (food and) water numerous offspring and long life. 6
हरदत्त-टीका
सूत्रम्

पय उपसेचनमन्नमग्निष्टोमसम्मितं सर्पिषोक्थ्यसम्मितं, मधुनाऽतिरात्रसम्मितं, मांसेन द्वादशाहसम्मित, मुदकेन प्रजावृद्धिरायुषश्च ॥४॥

  1. If an unlearned person who pretends to be (worthy of the appellation) ‘guest’ comes to him, he shall give him a seat, water, and food, (thinking) ‘I give it to a learned Brāhmaṇa.’ Thus (the merit) of his (gift) becomes (as) great (as if a learned Brāhmaṇa had received it).
