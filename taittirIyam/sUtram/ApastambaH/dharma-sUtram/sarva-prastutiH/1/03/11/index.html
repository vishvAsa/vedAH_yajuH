>
Bühler
  1. (The recitation of the Veda shall be interrupted for a day and evening if he has eaten), on beginning a fresh Kāṇḍa (of his Veda), food given by a motherless person, 1
हरदत्त-टीका
सूत्रम्

…{Loading}…

>
Bühler
  1. Nor is the recitation interrupted, if he has eaten rice received the day before, or raw meat (though these things may have been offered in honour of the dead), 4
हरदत्त-टीका
सूत्रम्

पर्युषितैस्तण्डुलैराममांसेन च नाऽनध्यायः ॥ ४ ॥

टिप्पनी

Pariṣad, ‘assemblage,’ means, in the language of the Śāstras, either a Pañc, an assemblage of learned Brahmans called together to decide some knotty point of law, or a Brahminical school, which studies a particular redaction of the Veda (see the Petersburg Dict. s. v.)
