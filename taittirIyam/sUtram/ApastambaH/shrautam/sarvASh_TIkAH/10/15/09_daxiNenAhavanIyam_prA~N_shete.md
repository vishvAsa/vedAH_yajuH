9. He sleeps with his face to the east, towards the south, the Āhavanīya.[^1] He does not sleep with his face downwards upwards. He should not turn his face away from the fire.[^2]  


[^1]: Cf. TS VI.2.5.5.  

[^2]: Cp. KS XXIII.5; MS III.6.9.
