A thread on taittiriya teacher list. All my acquaintances are āpastambins only (except one vādhūla). Apastamba, I the upākarma chapter just mentions kāņḍa ṛṣis must be propitiated followed by propitiation of sadasaspati in his usual cryptic manner. So, our own upākarma is very short. Tarpanams are offered to kāņda ṛṣis - prajāpati, soma, agni and viśvedevas. Then to 3 upanishads and to svayambhuva brahman (who's the ṛṣi of svādhyāya brāhmaņa). Then to sadasaspati but without the mantra "sadasaspatim adbhtam" (ideally should be used).

Tradition adds tarpanams to vedas, itihasa puranas and kalpa. Compared to long list of teachers in RV, SV and SYV (and hence lengthy tarpanam for those practitioners) taittiriya-āpastambīya is a short affair. Even the daily observance of devarṣi, kāņḍa ṛṣi pitṛ tarpanam is short - "devas starting with brahma... ṛṣis starting with kṛṣņadvaipāyana, and  to divine fathers...". 


However, we do see a ṛṣi and āchārya parampara in bodhayana dharma, hiranyakeśin gṛhya and bharadvāja gṛhya. The list more or less agrees among themselves except not all include satyāṣāḍha hiraņyakeśin. The deva, rishi and pitru list is comprehensive and bharadvaja example is here. Bharadvaja ends the chapter with "one must propitiate them day after day with waters" - indicating a pramana for deva rishi kanda rishi pitru tarpanams.

The rishi list is krishna dvaipayana (conflated with vyasa) -> jayukarnya-> taruksha-> brihaduktha-> trinabindu -> somashravas -> somasushmin-> vājashravas -> vājaratna -> varmin -> varūthin-> satvavān-> haryajvan-> vamadeva -> udamaya -> rinanjaya-> ritanjaya -> kritanjatlya -> dhananjaya -> babhru -> tryaruna ->  trivarsha -> tridhatu -> ashvayajna-> parashara-> vasishtha-> indra -> .. some devas .. -> sushravas -> satyashravas -> savitri, meters etc. 

Apart from this Rishi set, there's a separate set of teachers (not counted as Rishis) - Vaishampayana, phalingu, tittiri, ukha, Atreya the padakara,  kaundinya the vrittikara, kanva bodhayana (as pravachana Kara in some lists), bharadvaja the sutrakara,  apastamba and other sutrakaras and acharyas... (satyashadha hiranyakeshin added in at least one list).

Taittiriya tradition indeed has a strong set of teachers and ancient sages.
