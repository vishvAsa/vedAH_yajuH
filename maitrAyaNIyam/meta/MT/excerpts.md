## Boar
An early reflex of the myth of the divine boar bearing the Earth in the maitrAyaNIya saMitA of the kR^iShNayajurveda (in 1.6.3.3). It is a brAhmaNa on the use of the soil dug up by a boar as a ritual item:

> yAvad vai varAhasya chaShAlaM  
> tAvatIyam agra AsIt |  
> 
> yad varAha vihataM upAsya+agnim Adhatte | imAM eva tan nApArAT |  
> asyA enaM mAtrAyAm adhy Adhatte |  
> 
> tasmAd eShA varAhAya vimradate |  
> eSha hy asyA mAtrAM bibharti |

> As large as the snout of a boar, so large was this [earth] in the beginning. Since he [the sacrificer] establishes the fire after throwing up what was dug up by the boar, he does not go beyond this [earth]. He establishes it [the fire] on the very measure of this [earth]. Therefore, this [earth] yields to the boar; for it [the boar] bears the measure of this [earth].

> I suppose this is older than the AraNyaka reference in TA  
> uddRtAsi varAheNa kRSNena shata bAhunA

While that could be a later survival of an old incantation, as per our current understanding, of textual history, the version I cited would be older than the taittirIya one. The one from AV-paippalAda which I had mentioned earlier is probably even older.

## bibibAbhavann
A brAhmaNa statement from the maitrAyaNIya branch of the kR^iShNayajurveda:

> agnir vai sR^iShTo bibibAbhavann atiShThad asamidhyamAnaH |

> It is a brAhmaNa on the failed sAmidhenI deployment:  
"Indeed, agni, having been generated, kept flickering, not being kindled."

As a vaidika ritualist, one occasionally experiences this frustrating situation. The intensive verb used for this "bibibAbhavan" is probably the longest string of ba-kAra-s, aspirate or unaspirate, that I have encountered in a shruti vAkya. It is also striking for its diversity of "verbal" formations in one short sentence.

As a solution, it is said that prajApati used a shami samidh, and the same is recommended to the sacrificer in this situation.
