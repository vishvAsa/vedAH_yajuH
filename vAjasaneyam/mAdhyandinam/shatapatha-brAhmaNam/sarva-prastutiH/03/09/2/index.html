Eggeling
  1. That (Vasatīvarī water) is carried round for the sake of protection; Agni is in front (of the sacrificial ground), and now that (water) moves about all round, repelling the evil spirits. He puts it

down in the Āgnīdhra (fire-house) with, ‘Ye are all the gods’ share!’ whereby he makes all the gods enter it. It is a desirable object (vara) to the dwellers (vasat), hence the name Vasatīvarī 13, and verily he who knows this, becomes a desirable object to the dwellers.

  1. Now there are here seven formulas; with four he takes (the water), with one he puts it down behind the Gārhapatya, with one he carries it round, with one (he puts it down) in the Āgnīdhra,–this makes seven. For when the metres were produced from Vāc (speech), the one consisting of seven feet, the Śakvarī, was the last (highest) of them;–that completeness (he brings about): hence there are seven formulas.
