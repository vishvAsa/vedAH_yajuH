##  ०१
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

प्रजा᳘पतिर्वै᳘ प्रजाः᳘ ससृजानो᳘ रिरिचान᳘ इवामन्यत॥  
त᳘स्मात्प᳘राच्यः प्रजा᳘ आसुॗर्नास्य प्रजाः᳘ श्रिॗये ऽन्ना᳘द्याय तस्थिरे॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

1. Now Prajāpati (the lord of creatures), having created living beings, felt himself as it were exhausted [^egg_546]. The creatures turned away from him; the creatures [^egg_547] did not abide with him for his joy and food.

[^egg_546]: 217:4 Riricānaḥ, lit. 'emptied,' as āpyāy means 'to fill.'

[^egg_547]: 217:5 'Prajā' has likewise here the meaning of 'people, subjects, constituting the power or glory (śrī) of the king.
</details>


##  ०२
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

स᳘ ऐक्षता᳘रिक्ष्य᳘हम्॥  
अ᳘स्मा उ का᳘माया᳘सृक्षि न᳘ मे स का᳘मः स᳘मार्धि प᳘राच्यो म᳘त्प्रजा᳘ अभूवन्न᳘ मे प्रजाः᳘ श्रिॗये ऽन्ना᳘द्यायास्थिषते᳘ति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

2. He thought within him, 'I have exhausted

myself, and the object for which [^egg_548] I have created has not been accomplished: my creatures have turned away from me, the creatures have not abode with me for my joy and food.'

[^egg_548]: 218:1 For 'asmā u kāmāya' we ought to read 'yasmā u kāmāya,' with Sāyaṇa and the Kāṇva text.
</details>


##  ०३
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

स᳘ ऐक्षत प्रजा᳘पतिः॥  
कथं नु पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययेयो᳘प मा प्रजाः᳘ समा᳘वर्तेरंस्ति᳘ष्ठेरन्मे प्रजाः᳘ श्रिॗये ऽन्ना᳘द्याये᳘ति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

3. Prajāpati thought within him, 'How can I again strengthen myself: the creatures might then return to me; the creatures might abide with me for my joy and food!'
</details>


##  ०४
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

सो᳘ ऽर्चञ्छ्रा᳘म्यंश्चचार प्रजा᳘कामः॥  
स᳘ एता᳘मेकादशि᳘नीमपश्यत्स᳘ एकादशि᳘न्येष्ट्वा᳘ प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययतो᳘पैनम् प्रजाः᳘ समा᳘वर्तन्ता᳘तिष्ठन्तास्य प्रजाः᳘ श्रिॗये ऽन्ना᳘द्याय स व᳘सीयानेॗवेष्ट्वाभवत्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

4. He went on praising and toiling, desirous of creatures (or progeny). He beheld that set of eleven (victims). By offering therewith Prajāpati again strengthened himself; the creatures returned to him, his creatures abode for his joy and food. By offering he truly became better.
</details>


##  ०५
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

त᳘स्मै कमेकादशि᳘न्या यजेत॥  
एव᳘ᳫं᳘ हैव᳘ प्रज᳘या पशु᳘भिरा᳘प्यायत उ᳘पैनम् प्रजाः᳘ समा᳘वर्तन्ते ति᳘ष्ठन्ते ऽस्य प्रजाः᳘ श्रिॗये ऽन्ना᳘द्याय स व᳘सीयानेॗवेष्ट्वा᳘ भवत्येत᳘स्मै कमेकादशि᳘न्या यजते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

5. Therefore, then, let him offer with the set of eleven (victims), for thus he truly strengthens himself by offspring and cattle the creatures turn unto him, the creatures abide with him for his joy and food;--he truly becomes better by offering: therefore, then, let him offer with the set of eleven (victims).
</details>


##  ०६
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

स᳘ आग्नेय᳘म् प्रथम᳘म् पशुमा᳘लभते॥  
अग्निर्वै᳘ देव᳘तानाम् मु᳘खम् प्रजनयिता स᳘ प्रजा᳘पतिः स᳘ उ एव य᳘जमानस्त᳘स्मादाग्नेयो भवति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

6. In the first place he seizes [^egg_549] a victim for Agni. For Agni is the head, the progenitor of the gods, he is the lord of creatures: and thereby the sacrificer truly becomes Agni's own.

[^egg_549]: 218:2 Ālabh, to touch, seize, is a euphemistic term for immolating.
</details>


##  ०७
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ सारस्वत᳘म्॥  
वाग्वै स᳘रस्वती वाॗचैव त᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययत वा᳘गेनमुपसमा᳘वर्तत वा᳘चम᳘नुकामात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुत वाॗचो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते वा᳘गेनमुपसमा᳘वर्तते वा᳘चम᳘नुकामात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

7. Then one for Sarasvatī. For Sarasvatī is speech: by speech Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; speech turned unto him, speech he made subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by speech, and speech turns unto him, and he makes speech subject to himself.
</details>


##  ०८
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ सौम्य᳘म्॥  
अ᳘न्नं वै सोमो᳘ ऽन्नेनैव त᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः [^wbr_1] पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययता᳘न्नमेनमुपसमा᳘वर्तता᳘न्नम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुता᳘न्नेनो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते᳘ ऽन्नमेनमुपसमा᳘वर्तते᳘ ऽन्नम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥  

[^wbr_1]: प्रस्वामि (?) Sây.
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

8. Then one for Soma. For Soma is food: by

food Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; food turned unto him, and he made food subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by food; food turns unto him, and he makes food subject to himself.
</details>


##  ०९
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तद्य᳘त्सारस्वतम᳘नु भ᳘वति॥  
वाग्वै स᳘रस्वत्यन्नᳫं सो᳘मस्त᳘स्माद्यो᳘ वाचा᳘ प्रसाम्य᳘न्नादो᳘ हैव᳘ भवति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

9. And as to why it comes after that for Sarasvatī,--Sarasvatī is speech, and Soma is food: he who is incomplete by (having only) speech, now becomes indeed an eater of food.
</details>


##  १०
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ पौष्ण᳘म्॥  
पश᳘वो वै᳘ पूषा᳘ पशु᳘भिरेव त᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययत पश᳘व एनमुपसमा᳘वर्तन्त पशून᳘नुकानात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुत पशु᳘भिर्वेॗवैष एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते पश᳘व एनमुपसमा᳘वर्तन्ते पशून᳘नुकानात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

10. Then one for Pūshan. For Pūshan means cattle; by means of cattle Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; cattle turned unto him, he made cattle subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by means of cattle; the cattle turn unto him, and he makes the cattle subject to himself.
</details>


##  ११
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ बार्हस्पत्य᳘म्॥  
ब्र᳘ह्म वै बृ᳘हस्प᳘तिर्ब्र᳘ह्मणैॗवैत᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययत ब्र᳘ह्मैनमुपसमा᳘वर्तत ब्रह्मा᳘नुकमात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुत ब्र᳘ह्मणो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते ब्र᳘ह्मैनमुपसमा᳘वर्तते ब्रह्मा᳘नुकमात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

11. Then one for Br̥haspati. For Br̥haspati means the priesthood (brahman); by means of the priesthood Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; the priesthood turned unto him, he made the priesthood subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by means of the Brahman; the priesthood turns unto him, he makes the priesthood subject to himself.
</details>


##  १२
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तद्य᳘त्पौष्णम᳘नु भ᳘वति॥  
पश᳘वो वै᳘ पूषा ब्र᳘ह्म बृ᳘हस्प᳘तिस्त᳘स्माद्ब्राह्मणः᳘ पशू᳘नभिधृष्णु᳘तमः पुराहिता ह्य᳘स्य भ᳘वन्ति मु᳘ख आ᳘हितास्त᳘स्मादु तत्स᳘र्वं दॗत्त्वाजिनवासी᳘ चरति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

12. And as to why it comes after that for Pūshan,--Pūshan means cattle, and Br̥haspati the priesthood; hence the Brāhmaṇa (priest) has the most power over beasts, because they are placed in front (are protected) by him [^egg_550], because they are placed at the head (or in his mouth); therefore

[^egg_550]: 219:1 Purāhitāḥ (pura-āhitāḥ, Kāṇva rec.) has both the general meaning of 'put before him (as food)' and that of 'being placed next in order before him.'

having given all that, he walks clad in sheepskin [^egg_551].

[^egg_551]: 220:1 'Since the lordship over cattle belongs to the Brahman, therefore (the sacrificer) having given all his property to the Brahmans,' &c, Sāy.
</details>


##  १३
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ वैश्वदेवं᳟॥  
स᳘र्वं वै वि᳘श्वे देवाः स᳘र्वेणैव त᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययत स᳘र्वमेनमुपसमावर्तत स᳘र्वम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुत स᳘र्वेणो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते स᳘र्वमेनमुपसमा᳘वर्तते स᳘र्वम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

13. Then one for the Viśve devāḥ. For the All-gods mean everything (or the All); with everything Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; everything turned unto him, and he made everything subject to him. And so does this one now become strong by everything; everything turns to him, and he makes everything subject to himself.
</details>


##  १४
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तद्य᳘द्बार्हस्पत्यम᳘नु भ᳘वति॥  
ब्र᳘ह्म वै बृ᳘हस्प᳘तिः स᳘र्वमिदं वि᳘श्वे देवा᳘ अस्यैॗवैतत्स᳘र्वस्य ब्र᳘ह्म मु᳘खं करोति त᳘स्मादस्य स᳘र्वस्य ब्राह्मणो मु᳘खम्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

14. And as to why it comes after that for Br̥haspati,--Br̥haspati means the priesthood, and the All-gods this All; he then makes the priesthood the head of this All; wherefore the Brahman is the head of this All.
</details>


##  १५
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थैन्द्र᳘म्॥  
इन्द्रियं वै᳘ वीर्य᳘मि᳘न्द्रम् इन्द्रिये᳘णैव त᳘द्वीर्ये᳘ण प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययतेन्द्रिय᳘मेनं वीर्य᳘मुपसमा᳘वर्ततेन्द्रियं᳘ वीर्य᳘म᳘नुकमात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुतेन्द्रिये᳘णो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘द्वीॗर्येणाप्या᳘यत इन्द्रिय᳘मेनं वीर्य᳘मुपसमा᳘वर्तत इन्द्रियं᳘ वीर्य᳘म᳘नुकमात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

15. Then one for Indra. For Indra means power (indriya) and vigour; by power and vigour Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; power and vigour turned unto him, and he made power and vigour subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by means of power and vigour; power and vigour turn to him, and he makes power and vigour subject to himself.
</details>


##  १६
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तद्य᳘द्वैश्वदेवम᳘नु भ᳘वति॥  
क्षत्रं वा इ᳘न्द्रो वि᳘शो वि᳘श्वे देवा᳘ अन्ना᳘द्यमेॗवास्मा एत᳘त्पुर᳘स्तात्करोति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

16. And as to why it comes after that for the All-gods,--Indra is the nobility, and the All-gods are the clans (people); he thus places the food before him.
</details>


##  १७
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ मारुत᳘म्॥  
विशो वै᳘ मरु᳘तो भूॗमो वै वि᳘ड्भूॗम्नैव त᳘त्प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययत भूॗमैनमुपसमा᳘वर्तत भूमा᳘नम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नो ऽकुरुत भूॗम्नो एॗवैष᳘ एत᳘दाप्या᳘यते भूॗमैनमुपसमा᳘वर्तते भूमा᳘नम᳘नुकमात्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

17. Then one for the Maruts. For the Maruts mean the clans, and a clan means abundance; with abundance Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; abundance turned unto him, and he made abundance subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by abundance; abundance

turns unto him, and he makes abundance subject to himself.
</details>


##  १८
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तद्य᳘दैन्द्रम᳘नु भ᳘वति॥  
क्षत्रं वा इन्द्रो वि᳘शो वि᳘श्वे देवा वि᳘शो वै᳘ मरु᳘तो विॗशैवै᳘त᳘त्क्षत्रम् प᳘रिबृंहति त᳘दिदं᳘ क्षत्र᳘मुभय᳘तो विशा प᳘रिबृढम्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

18. And as to why it comes after that for Indra,--Indra is the nobility, and the All-gods are the clans, and the Maruts are the clans; he thus guards the nobility by the clan, and hence the nobility here is on both sides guarded by the clan.
</details>


##  १९
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थैन्द्राग्न᳘म्॥  
ते᳘जो वा᳘ अग्नि᳘रिन्द्रियं᳘ वीर्य᳘मि᳘न्द्र उभा᳘भ्यामेव त᳘द्वीॗर्याभ्याम् प्रजा᳘पतिः पु᳘नरात्मा᳘नमा᳘प्याययतोभे᳘ एनं वीॗर्ये उपसमा᳘वर्तेतामुभे᳘ वीॗर्ये अ᳘नुके आत्म᳘नो कुरुतोभा᳘भ्याम्वेॗवैष᳘ एत᳘द्वीॗर्याभ्यामाप्या᳘यत उभे᳘ एनं वीॗर्ये उपसमा᳘वर्तेते उभे᳘ वीॗर्ये अ᳘नुके आत्म᳘नः कुरुते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

19. Then one for Indra and Agni. For Agni means penetrating brilliance, and Indra means power and vigour; with these two energies Prajāpati then again strengthened himself; both energies turned unto him, and he made both energies subject to himself. And so does this one now become strong by both these energies; both energies turn unto him, and he makes both energies subject to himself.
</details>


##  २०
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ सावित्र᳘म्॥  
सविता वै᳘ देवा᳘नाम् प्रसविता त᳘थो हास्मा एते᳘ सवितृ᳘प्रसूता एव स᳘र्वे का᳘माः स᳘मृध्यन्ते॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

20. Then one for Savitr̥. For Savitr̥ is the impeller (prasavitr̥) of the gods; and so all those wishes become accomplished for him, impelled as they are by Savitr̥.
</details>


##  २१
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

अ᳘थ वारुण᳘मन्तत आ᳘लभते॥  
त᳘देनᳫं स᳘र्वस्माद्वरुणपाशात्स᳘र्वस्माद्वरुॗण्यात्प्र᳘मुञ्चति॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

21. Then finally he seizes one for Varuṇa; thereby he delivers him (the sacrificer) from every noose of Varuṇa, from every (guilt) against Varuṇa [^egg_552],

[^egg_552]: 221:1 Varuṇyād evaitat sarvasmāt kilbishād enaso ’ntato varuṇapāśāt prajāḥ pramuñcati, Kāṇva rec.
</details>


##  २२
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

त᳘स्माद्य᳘दि यू᳘पैकादशिनी स्या᳘त्॥  
आग्नेय᳘मेॗवाग्निष्ठे नि᳘युञ्ज्यादथे᳘तरान्व्यु᳘पनयेयुर्यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

22. Hence if there be eleven sacrificial stakes, let him bind Agni's (victim) to the one opposite the fire; and let them lead up the others one by one in the proper order.
</details>


##  २३
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

य᳘द्यु प᳘श्वेकादशिनी स्या᳘त्॥  
आग्नेय᳘मेव यू᳘प आ᳘लभेरन्नथे᳘तरान्यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

23. But if there be eleven victims [^egg_553], let them only immolate at the stake that for Agni, and after that the others in the proper order.

[^egg_553]: 221:2 That is, if there be eleven victims and only one stake, in that case Agni's victim is tied to the stake, and each succeeding victim is tied to the neck of the preceding one. Kāty. VIII, 8, 28.
</details>


##  २४
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तान्यत्रो᳘दीचो न᳘यन्ति॥  
आग्नेय᳘मेव᳘ प्रथमं᳘ नयन्त्यथे᳘तरान्यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

24. When they lead them northwards, they lead the one for Agni first, and then the others in the proper order.
</details>


##  २५
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तान्य᳘त्र निवि᳘ध्यन्ति॥  
आग्नेय᳘मेव᳘ प्रथमं᳘ दक्षिणार्ध्यं निवि᳘ध्यन्त्यथे᳘तरानु᳘दीचो ऽतिनी᳘य यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

25. When they throw them down, they throw down first the one for Agni, as the southernmost; then the others after leading them round northwards in the proper order.
</details>


##  २६
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

ते᳘षां य᳘त्र वपा᳘भिः प्रच᳘रन्ति॥  
आग्नेय᳘स्यैव᳘ प्रथम᳘स्य वप᳘या प्र᳘चरन्त्यथे᳘तरेषां यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>

<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

26. When they perform (offerings) with the omenta, they perform first with the omentum of Agni's (victim); then with those of the others in the proper order.
</details>


##  २७
<details><summary>मूलम् - Weber</summary>

तैर्य᳘त्र प्रच᳘रन्ति॥  
आग्नेये᳘नैव᳘ प्रथमे᳘न प्र᳘चरन्त्यथे᳘तरैर्यथापूर्व᳘म्॥
</details>
<details><summary>Eggeling</summary>

27. When they perform with those (chief oblations), they perform first with that to Agni; then with the others in the proper order.
</details>
