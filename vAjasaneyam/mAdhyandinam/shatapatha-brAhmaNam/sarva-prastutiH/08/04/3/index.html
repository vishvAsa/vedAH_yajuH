Prajāpati is about to perform the ‘sr̥shṭis,’ i. e. the creation of living beings by means of sacrifice (his own self). He requires the assistance of the Prāṇas (vital airs) in order to produce creatures endowed with breath, and he also appeals to (the three most prominent of) them in their capacity as R̥shis (VI, 1, 1, 1 seq.) to officiate as his (Udgātr̥) priests.
