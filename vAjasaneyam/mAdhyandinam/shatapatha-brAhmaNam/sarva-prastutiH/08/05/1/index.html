Eggeling
  1. Whilst meditating, they saw these Asapatnā (‘foeless’) bricks; they laid them down, and by means of them they made that world foeless, undisturbed; and because by means of them they made that world foeless, undisturbed, these (are called) Asapatnās.   8. 86:2 This is a doubtful rendering of ‘udyāma,’ which is accepted by the St. Petersb. Dict. for ‘shaḍ-udyāma,’ at VI, 7, 1, 16, 18; whilst in the present case ‘dakshiṇata-udyāma’ seems to be taken by it to mean southward erected, southward drawn (aufgespannt).’ Udyāma, in the sense ’extension,’ might mean a protruding part, serving as a handle. ↩︎
