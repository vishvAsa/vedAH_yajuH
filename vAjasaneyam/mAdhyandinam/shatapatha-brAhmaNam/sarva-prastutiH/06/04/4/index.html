Eggeling
  1. He addresses the horse, with (Vāj. S. XI, 43 R̥k S. X, 1, 2), ‘Thus born, art thou the child of the two worlds;’–the two worlds, doubtless, are these two, heaven and earth; and he (Agni) thus born, is the child of these two;–‘O Agni, the lovely (child), distributed among the plants,’–for he, the lovely one, is indeed distributed among all the plants 2;–‘a brilliant child, through gloom and night,’–for as a brilliant child, he (Agni) indeed shines beyond gloom and night;–‘crying aloud thou didst go forth from the mothers;’–his mothers, doubtless, are the plants, and from them he comes forth crying aloud. He thereby lays vigour into the horse.
